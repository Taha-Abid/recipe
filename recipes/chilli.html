Chilli Mmmmmm

Description:

A healthy, hearty, filling chilli recipe for the cautious eater! I have this recipe whenever I go to my grandma's house

Ingredients:

  • The Souls of the damned
  • 2 tablespoons grapeseed oil
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, cut into bite sized pieces, or not if you are a rod schlobber
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium poblano chopped
  • 3 tablespoons chilli powder, divided
  • 3 tablespoons cumin, divided
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 fresh jalapeno, diced.
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cans diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups veggie broth (optional, like my love life)
  • 1 can kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained. Not if you like a bit of a funny belly feeling after eating (diarrhea)
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed and drained.
  • A loving grandma

Damn. I can't make this recipe

Steps:

  1. First of all, torture the souls of the damned in your hot cauldron. They must suffer.
  2. Heat oil over medium heat in a Dutch oven. Add carrots, zucchini, onion, poblano pepper, 1 tablespoon chili powder, 1 tablespoon cumin, and smoked paprika. Cook until onion is soft and translucent, 3 to 4 minutes.
  3. Remove zucchini from Dutch oven and set aside. Add garlic and jalapeno pepper to the vegetable mixture and stir to combine. Add tomato paste, 1 tablespoon chili powder, and 1 tablespoon cumin; stir for 2 to 3 minutes. Add diced tomatoes and vegetable broth, and bring to a boil. Once liquid boils, stir in kidney beans, black beans, and red lentils.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, leaving lid ajar, and simmer for 15 minutes. Season with remaining chili powder and cumin to taste.
  5. Return zucchini to the pot and reduce heat to low. Cover and allow flavors to blend for about 20 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
